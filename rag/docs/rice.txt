RICE CROP ADVISORY GUIDE
Source: FAO Agronomy Guidelines for Rice Production

OVERVIEW
Rice (Oryza sativa) is a staple food for more than half the world's population. It is primarily a tropical crop requiring warm temperatures, high humidity, and abundant water.

OPTIMAL GROWING CONDITIONS
- Temperature: 20-35°C; ideal 25-30°C during growing season
- Rainfall: 1000-2000 mm per season (paddy rice requires standing water)
- Soil pH: 5.5 to 7.0
- Soil Type: Heavy clay or clay-loam with poor drainage (retains water)
- Water Requirement: 900-2500 mm per season (irrigated paddy)

PLANTING
- Sowing Method: Direct seeding or transplanting of 25-30 day old seedlings
- Nursery Period: 25-30 days
- Transplanting Spacing: 20x15 cm or 20x20 cm
- Seed Rate (Direct): 60-80 kg/ha; (Transplant) 40-50 kg/ha

FERTILIZER REQUIREMENTS
- Nitrogen (N): 100-120 kg/ha (3 split doses: basal, tillering, panicle initiation)
- Phosphorus (P): 50-60 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium (K): 50-60 kg/ha (basal + topdress)
- Zinc deficiency common in flooded soils — apply 25 kg ZnSO4/ha
- Excessive pesticides in flooded rice fields contaminate water and harm aquatic life

WATER MANAGEMENT
- Maintain 5-7 cm standing water during vegetative phase
- Drain field 10-15 days before harvest
- Alternate wetting and drying (AWD) technique saves 30% water
- Water stress at flowering causes severe yield loss (up to 50%)

PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT
- Major Pests: Brown planthopper (BPH), stem borer, leaf folder, gall midge
- BPH causes "hopperburn" — use resistant varieties; avoid excess nitrogen
- Do not use pesticides excessively in flooded fields — disrupts ecosystem
- Major Diseases: Blast, Bacterial leaf blight (BLB), Sheath blight
- Use certified disease-free seeds; apply tricyclazole for blast
- IPM recommended: light traps for stem borer, Trichogramma biocontrol

YIELD BENCHMARKS
- Low yield: Below 2.5 t/ha — drought stress, nutrient deficiency, or heavy pest damage
- Average yield: 2.5 to 5.0 t/ha — rain-fed or moderate input conditions
- Good yield: 5.0 to 7.0 t/ha — irrigated, well-managed cultivation
- Excellent yield: Above 7.0 t/ha — high-input, hybrid varieties

CLIMATE CHANGE ADAPTATION
- Rising temperatures above 35°C during flowering causes sterility
- Use submergence-tolerant varieties (e.g., Swarna-Sub1) in flood-prone areas
- System of Rice Intensification (SRI) improves yields with less water

POST-HARVEST
- Harvest at 20-25% grain moisture
- Dry to 14% moisture for safe storage
- Losses due to improper drying and storage can be 10-30%

SAFETY AND DISCLAIMER
All chemical pesticide applications must follow label instructions and local regulations. Avoid pesticide use near water bodies. Consult local agricultural extension officers for region-specific recommendations. Yield predictions are estimates based on historical averages.
