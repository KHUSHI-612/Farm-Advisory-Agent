SORGHUM CROP ADVISORY GUIDE
Source: FAO Agronomy Guidelines for Sorghum Production

OVERVIEW
Sorghum (Sorghum bicolor) is a drought-tolerant cereal crop, vital for food security in semi-arid and arid regions of Africa and Asia. It is the fifth most important cereal crop globally.

OPTIMAL GROWING CONDITIONS
- Temperature: 25-35°C; highly heat tolerant
- Rainfall: 400-600 mm per season; tolerates as low as 300 mm
- Soil pH: 5.5 to 7.5
- Soil Type: Well-drained loamy or clay soils; tolerates poor soils better than most cereals
- Altitude: Sea level to 2300m

PLANTING
- Sowing Depth: 3-5 cm
- Seed Rate: 8-12 kg/ha
- Row Spacing: 60-75 cm
- Plant Population: 100,000-180,000 plants/ha
- Sowing Time: At onset of rains or with irrigation

FERTILIZER REQUIREMENTS
- Nitrogen (N): 80-120 kg/ha (split: basal + top dress at 30 days)
- Phosphorus (P): 40-60 kg/ha (basal)
- Potassium (K): 20-40 kg/ha
- Sorghum responds well to organic manure (5 t/ha)
- Avoid excess nitrogen on poor-drainage soils

WATER MANAGEMENT
- Sorghum has excellent drought tolerance due to deep root system
- Critical water stage: flowering and grain filling
- Can survive short dry spells but yield drops significantly under prolonged drought
- Waterlogging tolerance is low — ensure drainage
- Total water requirement: 350-500 mm

PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT
- Major Pests: Sorghum midge, shoot fly, stem borer, aphids
- Sorghum Midge: Most damaging at flowering — use resistant varieties; spray cypermethrin
- Shoot Fly: Seed treatment with imidacloprid; early planting reduces incidence
- Major Diseases: Grain mold, downy mildew, charcoal rot, anthracnose
- Grain Mold: Major post-harvest quality issue — harvest promptly; use mold-resistant varieties
- Striga (witchweed): Parasitic weed — most serious biotic constraint in Africa
- Striga control: Use Striga-resistant varieties; apply ethylene to stimulate suicidal germination

YIELD BENCHMARKS
- Low yield: Below 1.0 t/ha — drought, Striga infestation, or poor soil
- Average yield: 1.0 to 2.5 t/ha — rain-fed smallholder conditions
- Good yield: 2.5 to 4.5 t/ha — improved varieties with good management
- Excellent yield: Above 4.5 t/ha — irrigated, high-input commercial farming

SPECIAL USES
- Grain sorghum: food and animal feed
- Sweet sorghum: biofuel and syrup production
- Forage sorghum: high-biomass animal fodder
- Sorghum is gluten-free — important for celiac disease patients

CLIMATE CHANGE ADAPTATION
- One of the most climate-resilient crops — ideal for drying climates
- Short-season varieties for erratic rainfall patterns
- Can be replanted after crop failure due to short season (60-90 days)

POST-HARVEST
- Harvest at 20-25% grain moisture; thresh when dry
- Dry to 12-13% for safe storage
- Grain mold is a major quality issue — store in dry, ventilated conditions
- Tannin-containing varieties have better storage life

SAFETY AND DISCLAIMER
Some sorghum varieties contain high tannins or dhurrin (cyanide compound in young shoots) — do not feed young green sorghum shoots to livestock in large amounts. All pesticide applications must follow label instructions. Striga management requires multi-season integrated approach. Yield estimates are based on historical regional data.
