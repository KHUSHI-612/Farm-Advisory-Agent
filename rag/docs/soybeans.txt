SOYBEAN CROP ADVISORY GUIDE
Source: FAO Agronomy Guidelines for Soybean Production

OVERVIEW
Soybean (Glycine max) is a high-protein, high-oil legume crop used for food, feed, and industrial products. It has the unique ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiosis with Bradyrhizobium japonicum bacteria.

OPTIMAL GROWING CONDITIONS
- Temperature: 20-30°C; frost-sensitive
- Rainfall: 450-700 mm per growing season
- Soil pH: 6.0 to 7.0
- Soil Type: Well-drained, fertile loamy soils
- Day Length: Short-day plant (requires <14 hours of daylight to flower)

PLANTING
- Sowing Depth: 3-5 cm
- Seed Rate: 60-80 kg/ha
- Row Spacing: 30-45 cm
- Plant Population: 300,000-400,000 plants/ha
- Seed Treatment: Inoculate with Bradyrhizobium japonicum before planting — critical for nitrogen fixation

FERTILIZER REQUIREMENTS
- Nitrogen (N): Minimal if properly inoculated (10-20 kg/ha starter only)
- Phosphorus (P): 60-80 kg/ha (critical for root nodule development)
- Potassium (K): 40-60 kg/ha
- Sulfur: 20-30 kg/ha (essential for protein synthesis)
- Calcium and Boron: Important for pod development
- Over-application of nitrogen suppresses biological N-fixation — avoid

WATER MANAGEMENT
- Critical stages: flowering (R1-R2) and pod fill (R5-R6)
- Water stress at flowering reduces pod set by 30-50%
- Total water requirement: 450-700 mm
- Avoid waterlogging — causes root rot and nodule death
- Drought at seed fill causes shriveled, low-quality seeds

PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT
- Major Pests: Soybean aphid, bean pod borer, spider mites, thrips
- Aphid: Spray imidacloprid at threshold of 250 aphids/plant
- Defoliators: Allow up to 20% leaf damage before treatment
- Major Diseases: Soybean rust (Phakopsora pachyrhizi), Sudden Death Syndrome, Frogeye leaf spot
- Soybean rust: Most damaging — scout regularly; apply triazole fungicide early
- Use disease-resistant varieties where possible
- Excessive pesticide use disrupts beneficial insects including bee pollinators

YIELD BENCHMARKS
- Low yield: Below 1.0 t/ha — poor inoculation, drought, or heavy rust
- Average yield: 1.0 to 2.5 t/ha — typical rain-fed conditions
- Good yield: 2.5 to 4.0 t/ha — optimized management
- Excellent yield: Above 4.0 t/ha — high-input precision agriculture

NITROGEN FIXATION BENEFIT
- Well-inoculated soybeans fix 100-300 kg N/ha — benefits subsequent crops
- Always rotate soybeans with cereals to maximize soil nitrogen contribution
- Crop rotation: soybean-maize-wheat is highly recommended

CLIMATE CHANGE ADAPTATION
- Heat-tolerant varieties for temperatures above 32°C
- Adjust planting date based on rainfall onset
- Drought-tolerant varieties for water-limited regions

POST-HARVEST
- Harvest at 13-15% grain moisture
- Dry to 12% for storage
- Store in cool, dry conditions to prevent mold and mycotoxin formation
- Premium grades require <2% damaged kernels

SAFETY AND DISCLAIMER
Raw soybeans contain trypsin inhibitors — always cook before consumption. Soybean rust spreads rapidly — early scouting is critical. Inoculant quality is critical — use fresh, certified Bradyrhizobium inoculants. Pesticide applications near flowering may harm pollinators — apply in evenings. Yield estimates are based on historical regional data.
