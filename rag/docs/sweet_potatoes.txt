SWEET POTATO CROP ADVISORY GUIDE
Source: FAO Agronomy Guidelines for Sweet Potato Production

OVERVIEW
Sweet potato (Ipomoea batatas) is a nutritious root crop grown widely in tropical and subtropical regions. It is an important food security crop due to its high yield, nutritional value, and tolerance to poor conditions.

OPTIMAL GROWING CONDITIONS
- Temperature: 20-30°C; sensitive to frost
- Rainfall: 500-750 mm per season
- Soil pH: 5.5 to 6.5
- Soil Type: Loose, well-drained sandy loam to loam soils
- Altitude: Sea level to 2500m with suitable varieties

PLANTING
- Planting Material: Vine cuttings (30-40 cm) from healthy mother plants
- Spacing: 30 cm within row, 75-90 cm between ridges
- Plant Population: 33,000-44,000 plants/ha
- Planting on ridges or mounds improves drainage and root development
- Avoid planting in waterlogged or compacted soils

FERTILIZER REQUIREMENTS
- Nitrogen (N): 40-60 kg/ha (excess nitrogen promotes vines over roots)
- Phosphorus (P): 40-60 kg/ha (promotes root initiation)
- Potassium (K): 80-100 kg/ha (most critical — improves root size and quality)
- Organic manure: 10-15 t/ha highly recommended
- Low-input crop compared to cereals — responds more to K than N

WATER MANAGEMENT
- Sweet potato is moderately drought-tolerant
- Critical water stages: vine establishment (first 30 days) and root bulking (60-90 days)
- Water stress during bulking reduces yield significantly
- Waterlogging causes root rot — raised beds or ridges are essential
- Total water requirement: 400-600 mm

PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT
- Major Pests: Sweet potato weevil (Cylas formicarius), whitefly, aphids, mites
- Sweet Potato Weevil: Most serious pest; use clean planting material; practice crop rotation
- No effective chemical control once infield — prevention is key
- Major Diseases: Virus complex (SPVD — Sweet Potato Virus Disease), Fusarium wilt, scab
- SPVD: Transmitted by whitefly; use virus-tested planting material; control whitefly vectors
- Fusarium Wilt: Soil-borne; rotate crops; use resistant varieties
- Avoid replanting in previously infected fields for at least 2 seasons

YIELD BENCHMARKS
- Low yield: Below 5 t/ha — weevil damage, virus infection, or drought
- Average yield: 5 to 15 t/ha — typical smallholder conditions
- Good yield: 15 to 25 t/ha — improved varieties with good management
- Excellent yield: Above 25 t/ha — optimized irrigation and inputs

NUTRITIONAL VALUE
- Orange-fleshed sweet potato (OFSP) is rich in beta-carotene (Vitamin A precursor)
- Important for combating Vitamin A deficiency in sub-Saharan Africa
- All parts edible: roots, young leaves (vegetable), and vines (animal fodder)

CLIMATE CHANGE ADAPTATION
- Drought-tolerant varieties suitable for changing rainfall patterns
- Short-season varieties (3-4 months) allow flexible planting
- Suitable as a catch crop after cereal failure

POST-HARVEST
- Harvest 3-5 months after planting when roots are mature
- Cure at 30-32°C, 85-90% humidity for 4-7 days to heal skin wounds
- Store in cool (15°C), dark, ventilated conditions
- Shelf life: 1-2 months with proper curing; roots deteriorate quickly without it

SAFETY AND DISCLAIMER
Sweet potatoes are generally safe to eat. Avoid consuming roots with heavy pest damage or visible mold. Use certified virus-free planting material to prevent SPVD spread. Weevil damage makes roots bitter and inedible — inspect at harvest. Yield estimates are based on historical regional averages and may vary with local conditions.
