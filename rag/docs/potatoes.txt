POTATO CROP ADVISORY GUIDE
Source: FAO Agronomy Guidelines for Potato Production

OVERVIEW
Potato (Solanum tuberosum) is the world's fourth most important food crop. It thrives in cool climates and is highly responsive to irrigation and fertilization.

OPTIMAL GROWING CONDITIONS
- Temperature: 15-20°C for tuber formation; above 30°C suppresses tuberization
- Rainfall: 400-600 mm per season
- Soil pH: 5.0 to 6.5 (slightly acidic preferred)
- Soil Type: Loose, well-drained sandy loam or loam soils
- Altitude: Grows best at 1000-3000m elevation in tropical regions

PLANTING
- Seed Material: Certified disease-free seed tubers (40-60g each)
- Seed Rate: 1500-2500 kg/ha
- Planting Depth: 10-15 cm
- Row Spacing: 60-75 cm; Plant spacing: 25-30 cm
- Earthing up: Critical at 20-25 days and 45-50 days after planting

FERTILIZER REQUIREMENTS
- Nitrogen (N): 150-180 kg/ha (split doses; avoid excess — promotes late blight)
- Phosphorus (P): 100-120 kg/ha (critical for tuber initiation)
- Potassium (K): 150-200 kg/ha (most important — improves tuber quality)
- Calcium and Magnesium: Apply if soil tests indicate deficiency
- Sulfur: 20-30 kg/ha improves protein quality

WATER MANAGEMENT
- Critical irrigation stages: stolon initiation, tuber bulking
- Water stress during bulking causes knobby/misshapen tubers
- Total water requirement: 500-700 mm
- Avoid waterlogging — causes tuber rot
- Drip or furrow irrigation preferred over sprinkler (reduces foliar disease)

PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT
- Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans): Most devastating disease; use metalaxyl/mancozeb
- Spray every 7-10 days in humid weather; use resistant varieties
- Early Blight: Apply chlorothalonil; maintain plant vigor
- Major Pests: Potato tuber moth, aphids, wireworms
- Tuber moth: Use pheromone traps; hill soil properly to prevent exposed tubers
- Colorado Potato Beetle: Apply spinosad or imidacloprid; rotate chemicals
- High pesticide use increases resistance risk — rotate mode-of-action groups

YIELD BENCHMARKS
- Low yield: Below 10 t/ha — disease pressure, poor seed, or drought
- Average yield: 10 to 20 t/ha — typical smallholder farming
- Good yield: 20 to 35 t/ha — improved varieties, good management
- Excellent yield: Above 35 t/ha — certified seed, irrigation, precision inputs

CLIMATE CHANGE ADAPTATION
- Use heat-tolerant varieties for warmer regions
- Shift planting dates to cooler months
- Mulching reduces soil temperature and conserves moisture

POST-HARVEST
- Cure tubers at 15-20°C, 85-95% humidity for 2 weeks before storage
- Store at 4°C for long-term, 8-10°C for seed potatoes
- Avoid greening (solanine formation) — store in dark conditions
- Green potatoes are toxic and must not be consumed

SAFETY AND DISCLAIMER
Green or sprouted potatoes contain solanine — a toxic alkaloid. Do not consume. All fungicide applications must follow pre-harvest intervals. Late blight spreads rapidly — early detection is critical. Yield estimates are based on regional historical data and may vary significantly with local conditions.
